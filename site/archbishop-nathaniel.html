    Biography

    An archpastor for North America

    Archbishop Nathaniel was born in Aurora, Illinois, in 1940, to a family of Romanian heritage from the County of Satu Mare who attended the Saint Michael the Archangel Romanian Greek-Catholic Church in Aurora. The experiences of his youth at Saint Michael’s awakened in him a desire to serve the Lord as a priest.

    Early studies — Illinois and Rome

    He attended Saint Procopius College, a Benedictine-operated liberal arts school and “Pontifical Eastern Rite Center” in Lisle, Illinois. Called to Rome by the Romanian Byzantine Rite Catholic Bishop VASILE (Cristea), he studied at the Gregorian University and resided at the Greek College of Saint Athanasius. There, the rituals, canons and practical spiritual life of the Byzantine Rite were learned and lived alongside students from Greece, Lebanon, Syria, Calabria and Sicily.

    During these years he was witness to the workings of the Second Vatican Council (1962–1966), and travelled extensively in Greece, Germany, Italy, and on to the mysterious and powerful encounter of Mount Athos. His comment at the time was, “If only the monks were not Orthodox!” It was on the Holy Mountain that one of the spiritual fathers bestowed upon him the name Nathaniel.

    Ordination and return to America

    Having completed his studies, he was ordained into the Holy Diaconate on 17 July 1966 at Colle de Tora, the “San Anatolia” summer chapel of the Collegio Greco, by Bishop Cristea. On 23 October 1966 he was ordained into the Holy Priesthood in the Romanian Uniate Church “San Salvatore” in Coppele, Rome, by the same hierarch. In January 1967 he returned home and was appointed Assistant Priest at his home parish, Saint Michael’s.

    Entry into the Orthodox Church

    The spirituality, theology and call of Orthodoxy, which had been dormant, began to beckon, and within months he laid aside the Unia and sought entrance into the Orthodox Church. Through Fr. Vasile Hațegan of New York and then Cleveland, he was introduced to Archbishop VALERIAN (Trifa), who worked with him to be received into the clergy of The Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America.

    He fully embraced the Orthodox Faith in the Chapel of the Nativity of the Birthgiver of God at Vatra Românească, Grass Lake, Michigan, in the presence of Archbishop Valerian, on 15 February 1968.

    Service as priest and monastic

    For several years he resided in a small monastic community on the Vatra property, and was later appointed rector of Holy Cross Church in Hermitage, Pennsylvania. An author of numerous articles, speaker and lecturer, he also taught at the summer youth camps of the diocese. He was Confessor to the Sisterhood of the Holy Transfiguration Monastery and was instrumental in the founding of an “Inter-Orthodox” Women’s Association in the Hermitage area.

    In April 1978 he was one of two representatives of the Orthodox Church in America at the Conference on Monasticism in Cairo, Egypt. He served as Spiritual Advisor to The American Romanian Orthodox Youth, and was an active member of the Liturgical Commission, the Late Vocations Program and the Episcopate Council. He is also a painter of “icons on glass” (icoane pe sticlă) in the Romanian peasant style, and a collector of historical art and artifacts.

    Call to the hierarchy — 1980

    At the request of Archbishop Valerian for assistance in the administration of the expansive diocese, he was called to the Hierarchy by a Special Congress of The Romanian Orthodox Episcopate on 20 September 1980. On the feast of the Transylvanian martyrs Sofronie, Visarion and Oprea, 21 October 1980, Father Popp professed the “angelic” vows (Little Schema) at the Vatra Chapel, taking the name of the Holy Apostle Nathaniel. He was then elevated to the rank of Archimandrite.

    His Consecration to the Episcopacy took place at Saint George Cathedral in Southfield / Detroit, Michigan, on 15 November 1980. The Holy Liturgy was officiated by Metropolitan Theodosius, Primate of The Orthodox Church in America, assisted by Archbishop Valerian of Detroit and of The Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America, together with:

    • Bishop Kyrill of Pittsburgh (OCA – Bulgarian Diocese)
    • Bishop Dmitri of Dallas (OCA)
    • Bishop Christopher of the Eastern Serbian Diocese (Serbian Patriarchate)
    • Bishop Boris of Chicago (OCA)
    • Bishop Mark of Boston (OCA)

    He was installed the next day as Bishop of Dearborn Heights, with the Saints Peter & Paul Church there as his cathedral, and was named Auxiliary Bishop for The Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America.

    Ruling Hierarch — 1984

    He assisted the Archbishop in this capacity until 1984, when Archbishop Valerian retired and relocated to Portugal. On Sunday, 17 November 1984, Bishop Nathaniel was enthroned at Saint George Cathedral as Ruling Hierarch of The Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America.

    Since then he has overseen the tremendous growth and expansion of the diocese — 39 new missions and parishes, a monastic community for women and a monastic community for men — carefully maintaining the Romanian tradition while integrating the many newcomers into American society, and at the same time recognising the importance of a united Orthodox witness in this land. He has remained at the forefront of the movement toward a united Autocephalous American Church.

    Pan-Orthodox ministries and humanitarian work

    His Eminence is:

    • Episcopal Moderator for the Pastoral Life Ministries Unit of The Orthodox Church in America
    • Chairman of the V. D. Trifa Romanian American Heritage Center in Grass Lake, MI
    • Organiser of the “Help for Romania Fund” and co-founder of “Help the Children of Romania” (1990)
    • Chairman of the Congress of Romanian American (CORA) since 1991
    • President of the Board of The Center for Orthodox Christian Studies in Detroit
    • Spiritual Advisor for the Orthodox Christian Laity (OCL)

    Pilgrimages and honours in Romania

    In 1994 he made a pilgrimage to visit all parts of Romania. In 1995, at the invitation of His Beatitude Patriarch Teoctist, he was a guest of the Patriarchate for the festivities celebrating 110 years since the Autocephaly of the Church of Romania and 70 years since the establishment of a Romanian Patriarchate.

    In May 2003 he travelled to Romania where he was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from the University of Oradea, in recognition of his leadership of the Romanian Orthodox community in North America and of his humanitarian efforts in post-communist Romania. Under his direction, the ROEA came to the aid of their brothers and sisters in Romania immediately after the fall of communism, and continues until this day to petition for their rights and to build mutual understanding with the Church of Romania.

    Elevation to Archbishop — 1999

    He has been an active member of the Synod of Bishops of The Orthodox Church in America since his Consecration, and has represented the OCA in numerous events both in North America and Europe. On Wednesday, 20 October 1999, in its Fall Session, the Holy Synod bestowed upon His Grace the elevation to Archbishop.

    May our Lord and Savior continue to bless His Eminence, Archbishop Nathaniel with good health, love and concern for his flock, and the vision of a greater Orthodox witness in North America.
